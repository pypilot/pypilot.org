pypilot supports almost any type of existing linear actuator.

A simple test would be to apply power to the wires in both polarities and ensure it moves both ways.
  • linear actuators
    • If attaching to a tiller pilot pin, you will need a thrust piece. One method is to cut a short aluminum pipe to have a slot and put a delrin round rod in it. This can be hose-clamped to the end of the linear actuator. A small hole can be drilled into the side of the delrin to snap onto the tillerpilot pin. These can also be 3d printed sample 1 sample 2
    • I really enjoy the following actuator on my boat. 3500N 300mm stroke 170mm/s speed 12V 24V DC Electric linear actuator
    • The following is recommended for more powerful drives Deluxe Rod Actuators
    • Larger linear actuator such as the raymarine type1/type2 reportedly work well with the mid/high power motor controllers with clutch outputs.
  • Wheel drives
    • Wheel drives can typically be driven using belt and pulleys. Some examples with pictures and video: drive1 drive2
    A 3D printed design for the wheel pulley
  • Hydraulic systems
    • Any type of marine hydraulic steering system can be made to work with pypilot. Typical systems include a reversing hydraulic pump controlled by the autopilot.
